## Material

WebSearch surfaced the leading vendors named repeatedly across multiple 2025/2026 round-ups (Otter, Fireflies, Fathom, Gong, plus Avoma, Grain, Granola, Read.ai). The search-result digest characterised positioning as follows:

- **Gong** — conversation-intelligence platform for sales/revenue teams; analyses calls, meetings and emails via its "Revenue Graph"; enterprise-grade with deep analytics and pattern recognition across many calls.
- **Fireflies** — AI notetaker for teams; multilingual transcription (100+ languages), CRM logging across Zoom/Meet/Teams/Webex, "AskFred" assistant; 50+ integrations, strong Salesforce/HubSpot automation.
- **Fathom** — AI meeting recorder for individuals and small teams; unlimited recordings/transcription on the free plan (AI summaries capped at five calls/month free); positioned as a budget-friendly Gong alternative.
- **Otter** — pioneer of the category; broad cross-platform capture (Zoom/Meet/Teams + in-person); stated up to 95% live transcription accuracy; round-up notes it has "fallen behind in features" and a federal class-action lawsuit was filed Aug 2025.

The fetched comparison page (index.dev, 2025-11-26) gave per-vendor positioning:
- **Otter** — target: multi-platform teams/enterprises needing real-time collaboration; headline: highest transcription accuracy at 95% with live collaborative editing; "the most reliable option" for multi-speaker calls.
- **Fireflies** — target: sales and customer-success teams using CRM workflows; headline: seamless CRM integration (Salesforce, HubSpot, Asana) with automatic task creation; "an automation nerd's dream."
- **Fathom** — target: small, remote, budget-conscious Zoom-first teams; headline: free, lightweight, instant Slack summaries; "completely free with no hidden costs."
- Article framing: "Each shines in its own lane … choose your tool depending on what is most important to you: speed, integration, or platform support."

Note: these are round-up/comparison characterisations (corroborating, not vendor-primary); accuracy and feature claims (e.g. Otter's "95%") originate from vendor marketing as relayed by the article.
