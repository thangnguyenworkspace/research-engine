# Selection — SQ-01

**Sub-question:** Who are the leading AI meeting/sales-call notetaker vendors and how does each position itself (target segment, headline capability)?
**Claim type:** competitive
**Deterministic trigger:** competitive / open-web claim → built-in Web tools. Vendor positioning lives on the open web (vendor sites, comparison write-ups, news); no specialised catalog source covers it.

## Candidates considered

| Source | Fit-for-claim | Verifiability | Access tier | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Web (WebSearch/WebFetch) | high — vendor positioning and the competitive set live on vendor pages, review sites, and round-up articles, exactly the open web's strength | medium — vendor self-positioning is primary for "what they claim," but round-ups are derivative; recency is good (24-month window readily met) | open | chosen |
| OpenAlex | low — academic graph does not catalogue commercial vendor positioning | low — wrong modality for a market question | open | rejected |
| World Bank | low — country statistics only, no vendor data | low — wrong modality | open | rejected |

## Chosen

- **Web (WebSearch/WebFetch)** — trust: corroborating. Will identify the leading notetaker vendors and their positioning; queried by WebSearch for the vendor landscape, then WebFetch on the most authoritative round-up / comparison result. Vendor self-claims are primary for positioning but the competitive ranking is corroborating (confirm against more than one page where possible).

## Rejected

- **OpenAlex** — wrong claim type; the scholarly graph does not index commercial product positioning.
- **World Bank** — wrong claim type; provides only country-level statistics.
