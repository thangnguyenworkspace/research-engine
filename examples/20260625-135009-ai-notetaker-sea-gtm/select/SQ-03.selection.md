# Selection — SQ-03

**Sub-question:** What does the research literature report about the reliability (word error rate / accuracy) of automatic speech recognition / transcription systems?
**Claim type:** technical
**Deterministic trigger:** research-basis technical question → OpenAlex. The question asks what the *literature* reports (peer-reviewed/preprint measurement of ASR accuracy), not what a vendor claims.

## Candidates considered

| Source | Fit-for-claim | Verifiability | Access tier | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OpenAlex | high — ASR word-error-rate research is exactly the scholarly graph's domain; returns titled, dated, DOI'd works | high — peer-reviewed/preprint primary works, citable by DOI, re-queryable by stable search | open | chosen |
| Web (WebSearch/WebFetch) | medium — benchmark blogs and leaderboards exist but mix vendor marketing with research | low-medium — derivative, often undated, vendor-incentivised | open | rejected |

## Chosen

- **OpenAlex** — trust: primary (for the existence and citation metadata of the research; the specific WER numbers inside are corroborating since they are study-specific). Queried at `https://api.openalex.org/works?search=automatic%20speech%20recognition%20word%20error%20rate&per-page=5`; record top works with title, year, DOI, cited_by_count.

## Rejected

- **Web (WebSearch/WebFetch)** — for a "what does the research say" question, the open web would surface derivative or vendor-incentivised summaries; OpenAlex is the more primary source. Reserved as fallback if OpenAlex is unreachable.
