# Selection — SQ-04

**Sub-question:** What does the research literature report about the reliability and known failure modes of automatic (LLM/abstractive) meeting or conversation summarization?
**Claim type:** technical
**Deterministic trigger:** research-basis technical question → OpenAlex. The question asks what the *literature* reports about summarization reliability and failure modes (e.g. factual consistency / hallucination), not a vendor's claim.

## Candidates considered

| Source | Fit-for-claim | Verifiability | Access tier | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OpenAlex | high — meeting/abstractive summarization reliability and factual-consistency research is squarely scholarly | high — peer-reviewed/preprint primary works, DOI-citable, re-queryable | open | chosen |
| Web (WebSearch/WebFetch) | medium — explainer articles exist but are derivative and uneven on rigor | low-medium — restates research without primary citation, recency uneven | open | rejected |

## Chosen

- **OpenAlex** — trust: primary (for the works' existence and metadata; specific findings inside are corroborating, study-specific). Queried at `https://api.openalex.org/works?search=abstractive%20meeting%20summarization%20factual%20consistency&per-page=5`; record top works with title, year, DOI, cited_by_count.

## Rejected

- **Web (WebSearch/WebFetch)** — derivative for a "what does the research say" question; OpenAlex is more primary. Reserved as fallback if OpenAlex is unreachable.
