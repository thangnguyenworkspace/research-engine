# Selection — SQ-05

**Sub-question:** What is the size of the business/enterprise base (number of registered firms or business density) in Vietnam and Indonesia that an SMB sales tool could address?
**Claim type:** quantitative
**Deterministic trigger:** quantitative country-level statistic → World Bank (primary owner of cross-country development indicators, including business-density series such as new business registrations).

## Candidates considered

| Source | Fit-for-claim | Verifiability | Access tier | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| World Bank | high — World Bank publishes business-environment indicators keyed by country and year (e.g. new business density / registrations, IC.BUS.NDNS.ZS and IC.BUS.NREG) | high — primary owner; exact, dated, re-queryable by stable indicator code | open | chosen |
| Web (WebSearch/WebFetch) | medium — national statistics offices and news report firm counts, but figures vary by definition and source | low — derivative, definitions inconsistent, often undated | open | rejected |

## Chosen

- **World Bank** — trust: primary (for whatever the indicator covers; note that the World Bank "new business density / registrations" series measures newly registered limited-liability companies, a proxy for the formal business base, not the total stock of SMBs). Queried at `https://api.worldbank.org/v2/country/VNM/indicator/IC.BUS.NREG?format=json` and `.../country/IDN/...`, taking the most recent non-null year.

## Rejected

- **Web (WebSearch/WebFetch)** — for a country-level business-base statistic the open web would restate a national-statistics-office or World Bank figure as a derivative; the primary owner is preferred. Reserved as fallback if the API is unreachable.

## Caveat (logged honestly)

World Bank's free indicators do not directly publish "total number of SMBs by country." The closest free, primary series is new business registrations / new-business density. This will partially answer the sub-question (a registration-flow proxy), not the full stock of addressable SMBs — flagged so the downstream claim is scoped to what the indicator actually measures.
